5. Asian Dowitcher

Limnodromus semipalmatus

The Asian Dowitcher is an amazing migratory bird known for its long, straight bill, which it uses to probe the mud for food like worms, crustaceans, and tiny fish. Every year, it travels thousands of kilometers between its breeding grounds in Siberia and its winter homes in Southeast Asia. This bird’s feathers help it blend into its muddy surroundings, making it an expert at staying hidden.

Despite its incredible migratory skills, the Asian Dowitcher faces threats from habitat loss as wetlands are being drained for development. Conservation efforts are focused on protecting these critical areas so that this bird can continue its long journey.

Fun Fact: The Asian Dowitcher travels thousands of kilometers each year, making it one of the most dedicated globetrotters in nature!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Asian Dowitcher
  • Scientific Name: Limnodromus semipalmatus
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 32-38 cm (12.5-15 inches) in length
  • Weight: 190-300 g (6.7-10.6 ounces)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years

6. Baikal Teal

Sibirionetta formosa

The Baikal Teal is a beautiful and colorful duck that loves to explore the shores of lakes and rivers. With its stunning plumage of bright green, turquoise, and rich brown, this teal is like a little rainbow on the water! Named after Lake Baikal in Siberia, where it first became famous, the Baikal Teal spends much of its time diving for food in freshwater habitats. It feeds on small fish, insects, and seeds, using its long neck to dive and forage underwater. During the winter, the Baikal Teal migrates to warmer areas, including parts of East Asia.

Sadly, the Baikal Teal is becoming rarer, mainly because of habitat loss. Wetlands are being drained for development, and pollution is making its home less suitable. Conservationists are working hard to protect the wetlands that the Baikal Teal depends on so it can continue to fly through the skies in all its colorful glory.

Fun Fact: The Baikal Teal’s bright colors make it one of the most beautiful ducks in the world—almost like a moving painting!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Baikal Teal
  • Scientific Name: Sibirionetta formosa
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 40-45 cm (16-18 inches) in length
  • Weight: 400-700 g (0.9-1.5 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 5-10 years

7. Seoul Pond Frog

Rana coreana

The Seoul Pond Frog is a tiny, charming amphibian found in the ponds and marshes of South Korea. With its bright green skin and adorable, big eyes, this little frog looks like something out of a fairy tale! It loves to hide in the mud and tall grasses of its watery home, where it catches insects with its long, sticky tongue. Though small, the Seoul Pond Frog is an important part of its ecosystem, helping control the insect population in its pond.

Unfortunately, this cute little frog is critically endangered. Its wetland homes are shrinking due to human development and pollution, and this frog needs help to survive. Conservation efforts are focusing on protecting its wetland homes and making sure the Seoul Pond Frog’s croak continues to be heard in South Korea.

Fun Fact: The Seoul Pond Frog might be small, but it’s an expert jumper! It can leap from one lily pad to another with amazing precision!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Seoul Pond Frog
  • Scientific Name: Rana coreana
  • Animal Type: Amphibian
  • Size: 6-7 cm (2.4-2.8 inches) in length
  • Weight: 10-20 g (0.35-0.7 ounces)
  • Lifespan: 5-7 years

8. West Caucasian Tur

Capra caucasica

The West Caucasian Tur, or Capra caucasica, is a mountain-dwelling goat-antelope found in the Caucasus Mountains. Known for its stocky build and large, curved horns, the West Caucasian Tur is a remarkable climber, navigating steep cliffs and rocky terrains with ease.

These herbivores graze on grasses, shrubs, and mosses, often moving in herds for protection. Males are larger and more robust, particularly during the mating season when they engage in head-butting competitions. However, their population is under threat from habitat encroachment, poaching, and climate change.

Conservation measures, including the establishment of protected areas, are vital for the survival of this unique species.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): West Caucasian Tur
  • Scientific Name: Capra caucasica
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 100 to 165 cm (39 to 65 inches) in length
  • Weight: 50 to 100 kilograms (110 to 220 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 10 to 15 years

9. Asiatic Lion

Panthera leo persica

The Asiatic Lion, or Panthera leo persica, is a subspecies of lion found only in the Gir Forest of India. Smaller than its African counterpart, the Asiatic Lion has a distinctive fold of skin along its belly and a shorter, less dense mane. Its tawny coat helps it blend into the dry forests and grasslands.

Living in prides, Asiatic Lions are social animals, with males defending territories and females hunting cooperatively. Their diet consists of deer, wild boar, and livestock. Despite their royal status in Indian culture, Asiatic Lions face threats from habitat fragmentation, human conflict, and disease.

Conservation efforts have been successful in increasing their population, but continued vigilance is needed to ensure their survival.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Asiatic Lion
  • Scientific Name: Panthera leo persica
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 170 to 190 cm (67 to 75 inches) in length
  • Weight: 110 to 190 kilograms (240 to 420 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 16 to 18 years

10. Persian Leopard

Panthera pardus saxicolor

The Persian Leopard, or Panthera pardus saxicolor, is the largest leopard subspecies, inhabiting the mountainous regions of Iran, Afghanistan, and the Caucasus. Known for its powerful build and striking spotted coat, the Persian Leopard is a solitary and territorial predator.

Its diet includes wild goats, sheep, and smaller mammals, which it hunts with stealth and precision. Sadly, the Persian Leopard faces numerous threats, including habitat loss, poaching, and conflict with humans. Efforts to establish protected areas and mitigate human-wildlife conflict are crucial for its conservation.

As an apex predator, the Persian Leopard plays a vital role in maintaining the balance of its ecosystem.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Persian Leopard
  • Scientific Name: Panthera pardus saxicolor
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 126 to 190 cm (50 to 75 inches) in length
  • Weight: 25 to 90 kilograms (55 to 200 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 12 to 17 years

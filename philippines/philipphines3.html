8. Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat

Dobsonia philippinensis

The Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat is an intriguing and rare species of bat that is found only in the Philippines. As its name suggests, this bat has a distinctive lack of fur on its back, giving it a naked appearance. It is a large bat with a wingspan of up to 1 meter (3 feet) and is known for its important ecological role as a pollinator and seed disperser. These fruit bats feed on a variety of fruits, particularly figs, and help to maintain the balance of the forest ecosystem by spreading seeds and encouraging plant growth.

While the Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat may seem strange due to its bare back, it is an incredibly important part of the biodiversity of the Philippines. Sadly, this bat is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting. Conservationists are working hard to preserve the species by protecting its habitats and reducing human-caused threats. These efforts include educating local communities about the importance of fruit bats and encouraging sustainable practices.

Fun Fact: Despite their large size, Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bats are incredibly agile and can fly for hours, covering vast distances in search of food!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name: Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat
  • Scientific Name: Dobsonia philippinensis
  • Animal Type: Mammal (Bat)
  • Size: Wingspan of 70 to 100 cm (28 to 40 inches)
  • Weight: 150 to 250 grams (5 to 9 ounces)
  • Lifespan: 10 to 20 years

9. Philippine Forest Turtle

Siebenrockiella leytensis

The Philippine Forest Turtle is a small and endangered species of turtle that is found only in the Philippines, specifically in the forests of the island of Leyte. This turtle has a distinctive appearance, with a dark, glossy shell and a slightly flattened body, making it look different from other species of freshwater turtles. The Philippine Forest Turtle is known for being a slow-moving herbivore, eating plants, fruits, and small insects found in its forest environment.

Unfortunately, this species is critically endangered due to habitat destruction, illegal pet trade, and hunting. The Philippine Forest Turtle is considered a "living fossil," as it has remained relatively unchanged for millions of years, making its preservation even more vital. Efforts are being made to protect its natural habitats and to breed the turtles in captivity for future release into the wild.

Fun Fact: Despite being slow-moving, the Philippine Forest Turtle has an excellent sense of smell and can find food buried deep in the soil or under leaf litter!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name: Philippine Forest Turtle
  • Scientific Name: Siebenrockiella leytensis
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: 25 to 30 cm (10 to 12 inches) in length
  • Weight: 0.5 to 1 kg (1 to 2 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 50 to 60 years in captivity

10. Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat

Crateromys australis

The Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat is a rare and fascinating rodent native to the forests of Dinagat Island in the Philippines. This nocturnal creature is notable for its large size, bushy tail, and distinctive coat of fur, which helps it blend in with the cloud forests of its mountainous home. The cloud rat is primarily herbivorous, feeding on fruits, seeds, and leaves, and plays an important role in maintaining the balance of the forest ecosystem.

This species is critically endangered, with only a small population remaining in its natural habitat. The Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat faces threats from deforestation, hunting, and habitat degradation. Conservationists are focusing on preserving the cloud forests of Dinagat Island and implementing captive breeding programs to save the species from extinction. With continued efforts, there is hope that the Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat can thrive once more in its native habitat.

Fun Fact: The Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat is an excellent climber and can swiftly navigate the trees and dense vegetation of the cloud forests where it lives!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name: Dinagat Bushy-tailed Cloud Rat
  • Scientific Name: Crateromys australis
  • Animal Type: Mammal (Rodent)
  • Size: 50 to 60 cm (20 to 24 inches) in length, including tail
  • Weight: 1 to 1.5 kg (2.2 to 3.3 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 10 to 15 years in the wild

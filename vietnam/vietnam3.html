8. Hawksbill Turtle

Eretmochelys imbricata

The Hawksbill Turtle is a sea turtle with a unique, beautiful shell that looks like it’s been painted with intricate patterns. This little sea traveler spends its life in tropical oceans, swimming through coral reefs and rocky coasts. Hawksbill Turtles love to snack on sponges, which are their favorite food, but they also eat sea anemones, algae, and jellyfish.

These turtles are critically endangered because of the illegal trade of their shells, which are used to make jewelry and ornaments. The destruction of coral reefs and getting accidentally caught in fishing nets also threatens their survival. Conservationists are working hard to protect their nesting sites and stop illegal poaching.

Fun Fact: Hawksbill Turtles are excellent swimmers and can dive as deep as 100 meters (330 feet) to find food!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Hawksbill Turtle
  • Scientific Name: Eretmochelys imbricata
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: 70-90 cm (27-35 inches) long
  • Weight: 45-90 kg (100-200 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 30-50 years
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

9. Chinese Pangolin

Manis pentadactyla

The Chinese Pangolin is a quirky, scaly creature that rolls up into a ball when threatened, using its sharp, protective scales as armor. It’s one of the most trafficked animals in the world due to the demand for its scales in traditional medicine. Found in forests and grasslands across China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia, this nocturnal mammal spends most of its time searching for ants and termites, which it eats using its long, sticky tongue.

Sadly, the Chinese Pangolin’s numbers are dwindling due to poaching and habitat destruction. To save these unique creatures, conservation efforts are underway to stop illegal hunting and protect their habitats.

Fun Fact: A Chinese Pangolin can stick out its tongue up to 16 inches (40 cm) to catch ants and termites hiding deep in the ground!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Chinese Pangolin
  • Scientific Name: Manis pentadactyla
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 40-70 cm (16-28 inches) long
  • Weight: 2-7 kg (4.4-15.4 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

10. Vietnamese Pond Turtle

Mauremys annamensis

The Vietnamese Pond Turtle is a small, freshwater turtle found only in Vietnam. With its smooth, dark shell and bright yellow-orange underside, it’s an eye-catching creature that loves to hang out in slow-moving rivers and ponds. These turtles are shy and prefer basking on rocks or logs during the day to soak up the sun.

However, this turtle is critically endangered due to habitat destruction and illegal collection for the pet trade. Conservation efforts focus on protecting the remaining populations and their habitats in Vietnam, ensuring these beautiful turtles can thrive in the wild.

Fun Fact: Vietnamese Pond Turtles are excellent swimmers, but they also love to spend time basking in the sun to warm up!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Vietnamese Pond Turtle
  • Scientific Name: Mauremys annamensis
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: 15-20 cm (6-8 inches) long
  • Weight: 0.5-1.5 kg (1-3 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 50-60 years
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

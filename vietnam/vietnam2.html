5. Siamese Crocodile

Crocodylus siamensis

The Siamese Crocodile is a freshwater crocodile found in Southeast Asia, mostly in Cambodia, Laos, and Thailand. Unlike its larger cousins, this crocodile is medium-sized, with adults growing up to 13 feet long. Its smooth, grayish skin helps it blend into its swampy home, where it waits quietly to catch fish, frogs, and small mammals. Siamese Crocodiles are nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night, stalking their prey in the dark.

Sadly, the Siamese Crocodile is critically endangered due to habitat destruction, illegal hunting, and the danger of mixing with other crocodile species. Conservationists are working to protect the few remaining Siamese Crocodiles by creating safe habitats and breeding programs.

Fun Fact: Siamese Crocodiles build nests out of plants and dirt, and the temperature inside the nest determines if the baby crocs will be male or female!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Siamese Crocodile
  • Scientific Name: Crocodylus siamensis
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: Up to 4 meters (13 feet)
  • Weight: 200-400 kg (440-880 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 50-60 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

6. Indochinese Tiger

Panthera tigris corbetti

The Indochinese Tiger is a magnificent and rare tiger subspecies, found in the forests of Southeast Asia. With its muscular build, it moves stealthily through the dense jungle, blending into the shadows thanks to its distinctive orange and black striped fur. This tiger is a little smaller than some of its relatives, but still mighty, with males weighing between 330 and 440 pounds.

Indochinese Tigers are solitary hunters, prowling alone at dawn and dusk, searching for deer, wild boar, and other mammals to feast on. They need a large territory to survive, and sadly, these tigers are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching. Conservation efforts are working hard to protect the jungle homes of these big cats and prevent illegal hunting.

Fun Fact: Indochinese Tigers have incredible night vision, which helps them sneak up on their prey in the dark!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Indochinese Tiger
  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris corbetti
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 2.5-3 meters (8-10 feet) long
  • Weight: 150-200 kg (330-440 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

7. Asian Elephant

Elephas maximus

The Asian Elephant is one of the largest land animals on Earth, known for its impressive size and gentle nature. With their huge, gray bodies and long trunks, these elephants are easy to recognize. They live in groups led by a wise, older female, who helps guide the herd through forests and grasslands in search of food and water. Unlike their African cousins, Asian Elephants have smaller ears and often have tusks.

These elephants are herbivores, munching on grasses, leaves, and even tree bark. But despite their size, they face many threats. Habitat destruction and human-elephant conflict, where elephants wander into farms, are just a few of the challenges they face. Conservation programs are working to protect these magnificent animals and help them live alongside people.

8. Chinese Egret

Egretta eulophotes

The Chinese Egret is a graceful bird with long, slender legs and stunning white feathers. Found along the coasts of China, Korea, and Japan, this elegant egret wades through shallow waters looking for fish, crabs, and small invertebrates to eat. With its long, sharp beak, it can pick up food from the water or mud with lightning speed. The Chinese Egret is often seen standing still in the water, waiting for its next meal to swim by, making it look like a statue in motion.

Unfortunately, this beautiful bird is at risk. Wetland areas are being drained to make room for development, and pollution is harming its delicate habitat. Conservationists are working to protect the Chinese Egret’s homes by preserving wetlands and educating people about how important these birds are for healthy ecosystems.

Fun Fact: The Chinese Egret is like a ballerina of the bird world, moving with grace and balance as it glides over the water!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Chinese Egret
  • Scientific Name: Egretta eulophotes
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 80-90 cm (31-35 inches) in length
  • Weight: 0.6-1.2 kg (1.3-2.6 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years

9. Great Bustard

Otis tarda

The Great Bustard is one of the heaviest flying birds in the world, with a wingspan that can stretch up to 2.5 meters (8 feet)! Native to the open grasslands of Europe and Asia, the Great Bustard is known for its grand size and majestic display during mating season. The male has an incredible, bright chest of feathers that puff up to attract females, making him look even more impressive. Despite their large size, these birds are capable of flight, though they prefer to stay on the ground, where they forage for seeds, plants, and small insects.

This magnificent bird is facing a tough future, with habitat loss due to farming and hunting pushing the Great Bustard toward endangered status. Efforts are being made to protect its natural habitats and help the birds thrive again.

Fun Fact: The male Great Bustard’s colorful chest feathers puff up like a giant balloon to impress potential mates—it's like a feathered fashion show!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Great Bustard
  • Scientific Name: Otis tarda
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 80-115 cm (31-45 inches) in length
  • Weight: 7-18 kg (15-40 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years

10. Greater Spotted Eagle

Aquila clanga

The Greater Spotted Eagle is a large, powerful bird of prey found in the forests, wetlands, and grasslands of Eastern Europe and Asia. With its sharp, hooked beak and keen eyesight, it can spot a rabbit, fish, or even a small bird from high up in the sky. These eagles are excellent hunters and spend much of their time soaring over their territory, searching for food. Despite their fearsome hunting skills, the Greater Spotted Eagle is also a symbol of strength and beauty in the wild.

Sadly, this majestic eagle is at risk due to habitat loss and hunting. Wetlands, where the eagle likes to hunt, are being drained, and the birds face threats from human activities. Conservationists are working hard to protect these incredible eagles and their habitats so they can continue to rule the skies.

Fun Fact: The Greater Spotted Eagle is an expert at flying high in the sky, often seen circling in wide, graceful loops as it searches for prey!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Greater Spotted Eagle
  • Scientific Name: Aquila clanga
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 60-75 cm (24-30 inches) in length
  • Weight: 1.8-3.5 kg (4-7.7 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 20-30 years

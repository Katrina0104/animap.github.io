1. Arabian Himri

Carasobarbus apoensis

Arabian Himri is a unique fish species found only in the upper parts of wadis in Saudi Arabia. These wadis are like dry riverbeds that fill with water during the rainy season. The Arabian Himri is a ray-finned fish, which means it has fins supported by thin, flexible bones. This fish is important to the people of Saudi Arabia because it is part of their natural heritage. However, the Arabian Himri is endangered, which means there are very few of them left in the wild. To help protect this fish, it's important to keep their homes clean and safe.

Fun Fact: The Arabian Himri is a ray-finned fish, which means it has fins supported by thin, flexible bones.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Arabian Himri
  • Scientific Name: Carasobarbus apoensis
  • Animal Type: Fish
  • Size: Up to 30 centimeters (12 inches) in length
  • Weight: Approximately 100 grams (0.22 pounds)
  • Lifespan: Up to 5 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

3. Asir Magpie

Pica asirensis

Asir Magpie is a special bird that lives in the highlands of southwestern Saudi Arabia. It has black and white feathers and a long tail, making it easy to recognize. This bird is important because it is the only bird species found only in Saudi Arabia. However, the Asir Magpie is endangered, which means there are very few of them left. To help protect the Asir Magpie, it's important to take care of their homes in the highlands and make sure they have enough food and space to live.

Fun Fact: The Asir Magpie is the only bird species found only in Saudi Arabia.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Asir Magpie
  • Scientific Name: Pica asirensis
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: Approximately 50 centimeters (20 inches) in length
  • Weight: Approximately 200 grams (0.44 pounds)
  • Lifespan: Up to 10 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

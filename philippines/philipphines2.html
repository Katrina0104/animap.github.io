5. Visayan Warty Pig

Sus cebifrons

The Visayan Warty Pig is a unique and critically endangered species of wild pig native to the Visayan Islands in the Philippines. Known for its distinctive warts on its face and long, bristly coat, the Visayan Warty Pig is a social and intelligent animal that lives in small family groups in the forests of the islands. These pigs are omnivores, foraging for roots, fruits, small animals, and even insects to sustain themselves. They play an important role in the ecosystem by helping to maintain soil health and promoting the growth of plants.

Sadly, the Visayan Warty Pig is at risk of extinction due to habitat loss, hunting, and the introduction of non-native species. Efforts to protect this species involve preserving their forest habitats, controlling hunting, and even reintroducing pigs to areas where they have become locally extinct. These pigs are an important part of the cultural heritage of the Visayan Islands, and their survival is critical for maintaining the biodiversity of the region.

Fun Fact: Visayan Warty Pigs have three distinct warts on their faces, which are believed to protect them from bites during fights with other pigs!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name: Visayan Warty Pig
  • Scientific Name: Sus cebifrons
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 60 to 75 cm (24 to 30 inches) at the shoulder
  • Weight: 50 to 80 kg (110 to 175 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 10 to 12 years

6. Philippine Cockatoo

Cacatua haematuropygia

The Philippine Cockatoo is a vibrant and charming bird native to the Philippines, easily recognized by its white plumage, bright yellowish-orange tail, and a striking red patch on its wings. Known for its intelligence and playful personality, this medium-sized parrot was once widespread across the Philippine islands. Unfortunately, the Philippine Cockatoo is now critically endangered due to habitat loss, illegal trapping, and trade, leaving it vulnerable to extinction in the wild. These birds primarily reside in forested areas, where they feed on seeds, fruits, and nuts.

The Philippine Cockatoo has an excellent ability to mimic sounds and can be trained to speak in captivity, although it is increasingly difficult to find them in the wild. Conservation efforts are focused on protecting their natural habitats and conducting breeding programs to bolster the population. Despite these challenges, dedicated conservationists continue to work hard to ensure a future for this charismatic bird.

Fun Fact: Philippine Cockatoos have the ability to create intricate nests inside large trees, where they lay their eggs and raise their young in complete safety from predators!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name: Philippine Cockatoo
  • Scientific Name: Cacatua haematuropygia
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 35 to 40 cm (14 to 16 inches) in length
  • Weight: 300 to 400 grams (10 to 14 ounces)
  • Lifespan: 40 to 60 years in captivity

7. Negros Bleeding-heart

Gallicolumba keayi

The Negros Bleeding-heart is a stunning and elusive bird found only on the island of Negros in the Philippines. Named for the red patch on its chest that resembles a "bleeding" heart, this bird is a member of the dove family and has a unique and mesmerizing appearance. The bird's feathers are mostly gray and white, but the vibrant red marking on its chest stands out sharply against its otherwise subtle coloring.

Once found in a larger range, the Negros Bleeding-heart is now critically endangered, with a rapidly shrinking population. The primary threats to its survival are habitat destruction and hunting.
